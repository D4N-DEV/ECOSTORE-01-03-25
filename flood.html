What is Before The Flood? What is its goal?


Before the Flood is a 2016 documentary film about climate change, featuring Leonardo DiCaprio as the narrator and producer. Directed by Fisher Stevens, the film explores the causes, consequences, and potential solutions to global warming. It was produced in collaboration with National Geographic and gained widespread attention for its urgent message.
The film follows DiCaprio as he travels to different parts of the world to witness the devastating effects of climate change firsthand. He speaks with scientists, activists, and world leaders to understand the scale of the crisis and what can be done to prevent further damage.


Major topics


Some major topics covered include:

Deforestation in Indonesia – Due to palm oil production, which destroys habitats and releases carbon into the atmosphere.
Rising Sea Levels in Miami – How coastal cities are already facing flooding due to climate change.
Melting Ice in the Arctic – The rapid loss of ice and its impact on global weather patterns.
Fossil Fuel Industry Influence – How corporations and lobbyists spread misinformation to delay climate action.

Notable Interviews
The film features conversations with key figures, including:
Barack Obama (then U.S. President)
Pope Francis, who speaks about the moral responsibility to protect the planet
Elon Musk, discussing renewable energy solutions like Tesla’s solar projects
Indian environmentalists, highlighting the challenges of balancing economic growth with sustainability


Reception


Before the Flood was well received for its accessible storytelling, compelling visuals, and urgent message. It was released for free on platforms like YouTube and National Geographic to reach a global audience. Critics praised its effectiveness in raising awareness, though some argued it oversimplified certain issues.

Climate change is already happening, and its effects are visible worldwide.
Urgent action is needed to reduce dependence on fossil fuels and switch to renewables.
